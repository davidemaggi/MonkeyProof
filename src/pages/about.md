## Welcome to my digital corner

I’m a System Architect at Accenture with nearly 20 years of experience turning coffee into distributed systems. I’m a full-stack developer by background, but these days I spend most of my time in the backend world — especially .NET — designing architectures that are meant to survive both scale… and developers.

This space is where curiosity becomes experiments, experiments become articles, and articles occasionally become things I later refactor.

## Who am I?

I’m Davide, and I’ve always been obsessed with understanding how things work. As a kid, I took apart toys. As an adult, I take apart architectures, legacy systems, and sometimes my own designs from six months ago.

After two decades in development, I’ve learned a few things: complexity is easy, simplicity is hard, and every “quick solution” eventually becomes a meeting. As a System Architect, I focus on building systems that are scalable, maintainable, and ideally not held together by invisible duct tape.

## What I do

### Backend & Architecture
Designing distributed systems, defining boundaries, and trying to keep microservices from multiplying after midnight. Most of my work revolves around .NET, APIs, messaging, and making systems behave in production.

### Full-Stack Perspective
I still enjoy working across the stack when needed. Understanding the full picture helps avoid architectures that look great on diagrams but cry under real users.

### Continuous Learning
Technology moves fast. Architects either keep learning or slowly become legacy systems themselves. I’m trying to avoid version 1.0 of me becoming deprecated.

## Nerd Territory

When I’m not thinking about system design, I’m probably somewhere in a sci-fi universe. I’m a big fan of science fiction in general, with a special place reserved for Doctor Who — time travel, clever writing, and solutions that are 80% intelligence and 20% running.

From space operas to dystopian futures, sci-fi has always influenced how I think about technology: big ideas, impossible problems, and occasionally fixing things with something that definitely shouldn’t work — but does.

## This blog

MonkeyProof is a place for **chaos, but technically accurate chaos**.

Here I write about software architecture, backend development, distributed systems, and whatever technical rabbit hole I’ve fallen into. Some posts are deep dives, some are experiments, and some are lessons learned the hard way so you don’t have to.

Think of it as notes from someone who’s been in the industry long enough to know that:

- There is no silver bullet
- Every system becomes legacy eventually
- And the simplest solution is usually discovered right after shipping the complex one

If you enjoy architecture, backend engineering, and technically accurate nerd chaos, you’re in the right place.
